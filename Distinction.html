    3. Neural Networks and Deep Learning: These are subsets of machine learning algorithms modeled after the human brain. They are particularly good at learning from large amounts of unstructured data like text and images. In the context of word and sentence training:

      • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) are used for sequential data and are therefore suited for text data, which is sequential in nature.
      • Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, a type of RNN, are effective in capturing long-term dependencies in text data which is important for understanding context in sentences.
      • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) can also be used for text analysis, although they're traditionally associated with image processing. They can capture spatial patterns in data and have been applied to sentence classification tasks.
      • Transformers, like the models underlying the BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) architecture, have recently become very popular for a variety of NLP tasks due to their ability to capture context from both the left and the right side of a word within a sentence.
